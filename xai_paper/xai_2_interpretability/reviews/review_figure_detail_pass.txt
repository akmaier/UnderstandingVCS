- Major: seriously hurts readability or credibility.

Global figure diagnosis

1. Too much prose is embedded inside figures.
- Many figures try to carry the title, thesis, data source, methods, caveats, legends, result summary, and interpretation inside the graphic itself.
- This creates overlapping text, unreadable micro-labels, and captions that repeat what the figure already says.
- Fix: each figure should answer one visual question. Move provenance, source paths, and secondary caveats to captions or supplement tables.

2. Minimum font size is too small.
- Several labels appear below practical print size, especially source lines, legends, callouts, and in-box subtitles.
- Target:
  - Main panel labels: 8-9 pt minimum in final PDF.
  - Axis ticks and method labels: 7.5-8 pt minimum.
  - Source/provenance text: move to supplement rather than printing at 3-4 pt.

3. In-figure legends are covering data or competing with data.
- Figures 2, 3, 4, and 5 place legends inside the data field or at locations that collide with labels, bars, or grids.
- Fix: place legends outside plotting areas, or replace them with direct facet labels.

4. Several figures use section references that are wrong or stale.
- Multiple captions and in-figure labels refer to "Section 1 oracle" or use "vs Section 1 intervention oracle" even though the oracle is defined in Section 3.2.
- Fix: replace all "Section 1 oracle" references with "Section 3.2 oracle" or simply "intervention oracle."

5. Figure captions are too long.
- Captions are being used as mini-results sections. This makes the visual page visually heavy and increases the chance of page-number/caption collisions.
- Fix: first sentence should state the figure's takeaway. Put detailed provenance and caveats in a compact "Data:" sentence or supplement.

6. Many data-source strings should not be in the figure body.
- Strings such as tools/xai_study/compare/out/leaderboard.json, P2-E6-1, faithful_demo.json, and experiment_design.md are unreadable at figure size.
- Fix: replace with "Data: committed records; see Supplement Table Sx." Put exact paths in the supplement.

7. Color/contrast accessibility needs a pass.
- Pale pink, pale gray, and light blue labels are hard to read against white or shaded backgrounds.
- Fix: use a colorblind-safe palette and ensure text contrast is high. Avoid using light-colored text for critical labels.

8. Method counts and figure numbering need consistency.
- Fig. 4 says "All 30 methods" while text elsewhere says 31 methods / 31 method rows.
- Page 30 figure header says "Figure 6" but the caption says Fig. 5.
- Fix: create a figure-number and method-count checklist before final compile.

9. The two taxonomy/map figures are overloaded.
- Fig. 5 and Fig. 6 are visually dense enough to be supplement figures unless simplified.
- Fix: either simplify them radically or move one of them to supplement.

10. Page layout needs a float-spacing pass.
- Fig. 5 caption collides with the page number on page 30.
- Fix: reserve enough vertical space for caption plus footer; consider a figure-only page, landscape page, or shorter caption.
