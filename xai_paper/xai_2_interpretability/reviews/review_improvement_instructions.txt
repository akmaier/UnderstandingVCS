Exhaustive constructive improvement instructions

Executive priority
- The paper has a genuinely interesting core: a known real software artifact can be used to audit interpretability methods, and causal faithfulness is not the same as semantic understanding.
- The present version should be revised as a benchmark paper first and a philosophical manifesto second. The benchmark must be reviewable, reproducible, and formally specified.
- The strongest revision strategy is to narrow the novelty claim, release the evidence bundle, define every metric, and make the semantic-gap claim precise without overreaching to all neural networks.

11. Tighten the semantic-gap definition.
- Define "semantic gap" operationally as the residual between causal-structure recovery and algorithmic/semantic recovery.
- Avoid implying a single scalar if you only measure three separations. Better:
  - "We operationalize the gap through three separations: faithful-not-sufficient, named-not-causal, decodable-not-used."
- Give each separation a formal diagnostic:
  - Graph-correct but behavior-incomplete: high F/M, low S.
  - Named but causally inert: high matched fraction, low causal F.
  - Decodable but unused: high probe accuracy/selectivity, low intervention effect.
- State explicitly that these diagnose semantic failure modes, not a complete theory of semantics.

12. Scope the neural-network transfer claim.
