\section{Claims and evidence}\label{supp:claims}

Every headline claim in the paper rests on a specific artifact. The table in this section
makes that dependency checkable. For each claim we list the figure or table that supports it,
the games and methods it uses, the metric, the artifact it traces to, and the limitation that
bounds it. Faithfulness, sufficiency, and minimality are the three axes of \Sectionname\ref{sec:triad}.
The numbers themselves are sourced in \Sectionname\ref{supp:provenance}. The table below names which
evidence supports which claim; it does not repeat the values. All aggregate scores are taken
over the 42-game scored set of \Sectionname\ref{supp:scoredset}. One split matters for how the
table is read. The all-regime faithfulness gap between causal/intervention and
gradient/correlational methods is $0.321$ (causal/intervention mean $0.705$ vs.\ gradient/correlational
$0.384$), and it is the primary evidence for the headline. The position-only gap is now
significant on this set: bootstrapping the family-mean gap over the 42 games gives a mean of
$0.327$ with a $95\%$ interval of $[0.277,0.375]$ that excludes zero. At the earlier six-game
scope this gap was $0.238$ with an interval of $[-0.049,0.315]$ that crossed zero; scaling to
42 games resolves it into a significant result.
