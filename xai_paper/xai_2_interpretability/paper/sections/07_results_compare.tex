\section{Across the traditions --- and the boundary the leaderboard cannot
cross}\label{sec:results_compare}

We have now run three traditions on one machine, and the scores live on a single
axis. It is worth pausing to ask what the axis measures and what it does not.
Faithfulness asks whether an explanation names the true causes; on the VCS we can
answer it exactly, because the \S\ref{sec:oracle} intervention oracle re-runs the
bit-exact program for every candidate cause and returns the ground truth no real
network affords. In this section we first place all of Phases~A--C on the shared
faithfulness-versus-plausibility plane and read off the leaderboard and its danger
zone---the \emph{confirm} half of the paper. We then do the thing the leaderboard
cannot: with the same oracle, we mark the boundary where even a perfectly faithful
method stops. Faithfulness, it turns out, is the easier half of the problem. The
harder half---the variable's meaning, the routine's algorithm---is a separate
object, and on this machine the distance to it is a number. We name that residual
the \emph{semantic gap} and measure its symptoms.

\subsection*{The faithfulness leaderboard: causal wins, correlational loses}

The headline is a clean separation along the axis that matters. Aggregating the
257 committed per-game records into 31 method rows~\cite{leaderboardP2}
(Fig.~\ref{fig:faithfulness}), the causal and intervention methods average a
faithfulness of $0.681$ against the oracle, while the gradient and correlational
methods average $0.294$---a gap of $0.387$ across all output regimes. Restrict to
the \emph{position} regime, the discrete sprite-position outputs whose naive
gradient is provably zero (Sec.~\ref{sec:results-b}), and the gap holds with the
same sign: causal/intervention $0.412$ versus gradient/correlational $0.068$. The
ordering is the one the method matrix predicted (Sec.~\ref{sec:methods}), now
measured: activation patching, causal scrubbing, interchange interventions, and
the logit lens sit at the oracle ceiling of $1.0$; the single-unit lesion follows
at $0.99$; the gradient family and the classical correlational analyses---tuning
curves, Granger causality, spike-word correlations---fall to the floor. The single
starkest pair makes the spread concrete and is bit-exact on every game: on the
position regime the causal method activation patching reaches faithfulness $1.000$,
the oracle ceiling, on all six core games, while vanilla saliency---the field's
default tool---collapses to $0.000$ on all six, a per-method gap of
$1.000$~\cite{faithfuldemoP2}. On a system where the answer is known exactly, the
everyday attribution tool scores near chance where a causal method scores near the
ceiling.

\subsection*{The danger zone: plausible is not faithful}

The faithfulness axis would be unremarkable if it agreed with how convincing a
method looks. It does not. Plotting the same methods against the
plausibility proxy of Sec.~\ref{sec:methods}, the cloud separates into a benign
diagonal and a hazardous corner. The methods the field reaches for first---the
gradient family, plausibility $0.9$; tuning and Granger, plausibility $0.8$;
linear probing with control tasks, plausibility $0.85$---are precisely the methods
whose faithfulness sits near the floor. Expected Gradients tops the danger list at
plausibility $0.9$ against faithfulness $0.034$, a gap of $0.87$; linear probing
follows at $0.85$ against $0.162$; A3 tuning at $0.8$ against $0.116$. The
inversion is the whole point: the map that is provably empty on the position
regime looks \emph{more} convincing than the map that is exactly right. We state
the obvious caveat plainly. The plausibility axis is a documented
method-tradition proxy, not a human-subjects measurement, and we report it as such
to avoid overclaiming the danger zone. Yet the faithfulness axis is exact, and the
ranking it inverts is the field's own habit of trust; the hazard is therefore real
even at the proxy's coarse resolution. The danger zone is not a worry on the VCS.
It is a measurement.

\subsection*{Where the leaderboard stops --- the semantic gap}

Here the paper turns. Suppose we hand the audit its best case: a method that scores
$1.0$ against the oracle, a faithfulness no real network can certify. Has it given
us an understanding of what the program does? We claim, and now show, that it has
not---and we are careful about what that claim is and is not. We do \emph{not}
claim that no method can recover meaning; that is the unbounded statement, and it
is the subject of the companion design-recovery work (Paper~4), which we have not
run here. We claim the bounded thing the oracle can prove: even when a method
recovers the true causal structure exactly, what it returns is the \emph{wiring}
---a causal-effect map, a data-flow graph---and not the \emph{semantics}: the
variable's meaning or the routine's algorithm. The missing object is of a
different type, not a worse-fit instance of the same one. The triad of
Sec.~\ref{sec:triad} makes this operational: an explanation is right only if it is
\textbf{F}aithful (true causes), \textbf{S}ufficient (it regenerates the behavior
under held-out interventions), and \textbf{M}inimal (at the algorithmic level).
Three committed separations, each adjudicated by the oracle rather than by our
preference, show the axes pulling apart---and, tellingly, pulling apart in
\emph{both directions at once} (Fig.~\ref{fig:taxonomy}).

\paragraph{Graph-correct, behavior-incomplete.}
Automatic circuit discovery (ACDC)~\cite{conmy2023towards} on Breakout recovers a
data-flow graph that is, by the exact data-flow oracle, perfectly correct and
perfectly parsimonious: $F = 1.0$ and $M = 1.0$~\cite{leaderboardP2}. And yet the
discovered circuit alone, with its non-circuit parents scrubbed, reproduces the
clean candidate-cell readouts only $44\%$ of the time: $S = 0.44$. A correct graph
of which cells feed which is not the function those cells compute. The companion
causal method shows the same thing from the faithful side: activation
patching~\cite{vig2020investigating} recovers the exact per-site causal-effect
table on every one of the six games---the record carries
\texttt{recovered\_equals\_exact: true} bit-for-bit~\cite{leaderboardP2}---and a
table of effects, however exact, is not the bounce algorithm that produced them.
Crucially, there is no tuning budget left to spend here: the effect table is
\emph{already} exact, so the residual to the algorithm cannot be a worse fit closed
by a better hyperparameter. It is a missing object of a different type. This is the
sharpest reading of the triad and the reason the gap is not merely
``methods underperform.''

\paragraph{Named, yet causally inert.}
The reverse failure is just as instructive. A sparse
autoencoder~\cite{cunningham2023sparse} trained on the recorded Pong state matches
every one of its candidate game-concept variables: the feature$\leftrightarrow$
variable matched fraction is $1.0$, a perfect semantic alignment by the field's
usual yardstick. By the oracle, those same matched features are nearly causally
inert: faithfulness $F = 0.041$ and a \emph{negative} sufficiency $S =
-0.336$~\cite{leaderboardP2}. ``Looks like the ball's $x$'' is not ``is how the
program computes with the ball's $x$.'' Where the circuit was correct yet
incomplete, the dictionary is named yet wrong---the gap runs the other way on the
same axis, and a story in which methods simply score too low cannot produce both at
once. We add the obvious guard against reading semantic success into an aligned
variable: the interchange-intervention alignment (DAS)~\cite{geiger2023finding}
reports an aligned-variable accuracy of $1.0$, but that $1.0$ is true \emph{by
construction}---it verifies a \emph{supplied} variable rather than discovering one.
A method that confirms a given concept is not a method that found the meaning.

\paragraph{Decodable, yet unused.}
The third separation is the present-versus-used trap, and the oracle is strictly
stronger than the probe that walks into it. Linear probing with control
tasks~\cite{hewitt2019designing} decodes game concepts from the Breakout state at a
mean accuracy of $0.72$ with positive selectivity over its control---the
information is demonstrably \emph{present}. Yet the exact oracle flags a decodable
score-related cell (cell~$84$) as \emph{not causally used}: re-derived within the
horizon, its value has no downstream effect on the
output~\cite{leaderboardP2}. A concept can be linearly readable from the state and
play no part in the computation, exactly Hewitt and Liang's control-task
warning---decodability is necessary, never sufficient. Probing answers ``is it
there''; the oracle answers ``is it used,'' and only the second is about how the
program works. Linear probing's leaderboard faithfulness of $0.162$ paired with a
plausibility proxy of $0.85$ is its place in the danger zone, and the
decodable-but-unused cell is the mechanism behind that placement.

\paragraph{The gap is a number, and a different kind of object.}
Read together (the leaderboard plane of Fig.~\ref{fig:faithfulness} and the
failure taxonomy of Fig.~\ref{fig:taxonomy}), the three separations make the same
point from three sides. The triad's axes are not redundant: a method can be faithful and minimal
yet not sufficient (ACDC, $F = M = 1.0$, $S = 0.44$); it can match a named concept
perfectly yet be causally inert (the SAE, matched fraction $1.0$, $F = 0.041$); it
can decode a variable that the program never uses (cell~$84$). The robustness of
the claim does not rest on any single low number---a lone low $S$ could be a
tuning artifact---but on the gap appearing where tuning \emph{cannot} reach (an
already-exact effect table) and running in opposite directions on the same axis
at once. That two-sidedness is what a ``methods are not good enough yet'' account
cannot generate. We restate the honesty contract here, because the temptation to
overread is greatest at the paper's centre. We have \emph{not} recovered the
program's meaning, algorithm, variables, or design from scratch. Every semantic
label we used is external---imported from AtariARI~\cite{anand2019unsupervised} and
OCAtari~\cite{delfosse2023ocatari}, \texttt{verified:false} in provenance---and
the substrate only \emph{causally verifies} a supplied label; it does not
\emph{discover} the concept. The faithful methods recover the wiring; the meaning
is a separate object, and the distance to it is what we have measured. Recovering
that meaning is a different problem, and it is the companion paper's, not this
one's.
