\section{Methods}\label{sec:methods}

Our method has a single organizing idea: we study an interpretability question on
a machine whose answer we can compute exactly. Everything below serves that idea.
We first describe the substrate that makes the ground truth obtainable, then the
oracle that delivers it, then the tiered semantics we attach to it, and finally the
correctness triad that turns ``do we understand?'' into a set of numbers. The full
per-method setups live in the Supplementary Information; here we give the measurement
instrument and the scoring, which are shared by all three phases. Figure~\ref{fig:platform}
sketches the whole pipeline --- substrate, oracle, and triad --- in one schematic.

\subsection{The differentiable, bit-exact VCS substrate}\label{sec:substrate}

The subject of this study is the Atari Video Computer System (VCS) itself --- its chip,
its program, and its game logic --- not a learned agent. We require a complex, real
artifact that was never designed to be interpretable, yet whose every causal dependency
we can read off exactly. Paper~1 built precisely this: a bit-exact, differentiable port
of the 1977 VCS, validated bit-for-bit against the \texttt{xitari} C++ reference on all
64 games of the Paper-1 roster, in both random-access memory and rendered screen, within
the validated conformance window~\cite{maier2025vcs,xitari}. We use that port in two
interchangeable realizations --- \texttt{jutari} (Julia) and \texttt{jaxtari}
(JAX/Python) --- which are themselves cross-checked to be identical.

The substrate grants four capabilities that no neural network offers for free, and on
which the rest of the method rests. \emph{First}, an exact forward map. We write the
machine's behavior as
\begin{equation}
  y \;=\; \mathrm{VCS}\big(\mathrm{ROM},\, \mathrm{inputs}[0{:}t],\, s_0\big),
  \label{eq:vcs}
\end{equation}
that is, the output $y$ at step $t$ --- a pixel, a score, or a game event --- is a
deterministic function of the cartridge bytes, the joystick and switch inputs up to $t$,
and the initial state $s_0$. Re-running with the same arguments reproduces $y$ to the
bit. \emph{Second}, exact interventions. We can set any cause $u$ --- a ROM byte, a RAM
cell, a CPU/TIA/RIOT register, an opcode, or a joystick input --- to any value and re-run
Eq.~\eqref{eq:vcs} deterministically, which realizes the causal operator $\mathrm{do}(u)$
on a real machine rather than on an assumed world model. \emph{Third}, full state
observability: every bit of RAM and every register is recorded along the trajectory, so
the ``activations'' we interpret are the program's own variables. \emph{Fourth}, a
content-path gradient $\partial y / \partial u$ through a straight-through estimator (STE)
formulation whose forward pass is bit-exact to the hard machine at any finite
temperature~\cite{bengio2013estimating,jang2017gumbel}, and which we batch on the GPU by
vectorized mapping. The three observable faces of Eq.~\eqref{eq:vcs} --- inputs we drive,
internal state we read and clamp, and outputs we explain --- are the same three faces a
neural interpretability study works with, which is what lets one substrate carry the
neuroscience, attribution, and mechanistic-interpretability batteries at once.

We honor one boundary throughout. Paper-1 bit-exactness holds within a bounded frame
window on a fixed action stream (on the order of 30 frames of RAM and 60 frames of
screen); every scored claim is taken inside that validated horizon, or the conformance is
re-established before scoring. Long-horizon end-to-end gradients are not needed for the
single-step metrics we report, so we do not assume them.

\subsection{The ground-truth oracle}\label{sec:oracle}

The oracle is the heart of the method. For any output $y$ and any candidate cause $u$ it
returns the \emph{true} effect of $u$ on $y$, against which every interpretability method
is then scored.

The primary construction is the \emph{exact intervention oracle}. We intervene on $u$ ---
occlude, clamp, replace, or resample it --- re-run the machine deterministically, and
record the change in the output,
\begin{equation}
  \Delta y(u) \;=\; \mathrm{VCS}\big(\dots,\, \mathrm{do}(u{:=}v'),\, \dots\big) \;-\; y .
  \label{eq:doy}
\end{equation}
In words: the true causal effect of a cause is the bit-exact difference its surgical
edit makes to the output, measured by actually running the altered machine. No
world-model and no smoothness are assumed; each $\Delta y$ is a real re-run, and the
collection $\{\Delta y(u)\}$ over all causes is the true causal map of $y$. This is the
reference for the faithfulness axis of every method in the paper.

The companion construction is the \emph{content-path gradient}, $\partial y / \partial u$,
read through the differentiable substrate. It is fast and dense, and it agrees with the
intervention oracle on the content path --- the register, color, and graphics-bit values
that flow smoothly into the pixel. \emph{Yet,} a single, decisive caveat carries through
the entire study and we state it plainly. The STE gradient routes to the content path
only; a \emph{discrete index} --- a sprite's screen position, which the VCS sets by the
timing of a strobe write --- has \emph{zero naive gradient}, because an infinitesimal
change of the cause never crosses the integer boundary that moves the sprite. Recovering
a position gradient at all requires the bilinear sampler of Paper~1~\cite{jaderberg2015spatial}.
\emph{Hence,} for content outputs the gradient is a legitimate companion oracle, while
for \emph{position, index, and event} outputs the intervention oracle of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:doy} is the \emph{sole} ground truth, and every gradient-based method is
scored \emph{as a method under test}, not promoted to oracle. This single distinction ---
the \emph{position regime} --- is where the sharpest separations in our results appear.
Where the two constructions overlap on the content path we report their correlation and
flag, rather than hide, the non-smooth and saturated points at which they disagree.

\subsection{Tiered ground truth and how we obtain it}\label{sec:tiers}

The ground truth comes in three tiers, and the tiering is not bookkeeping --- it carries
the central argument of the paper. \emph{Tier~1 (T1)} is the exact causal map of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:doy}, derived internally from the substrate and available for all 64 games.
\emph{Tier~2 (T2)} is the hardware roles and the read/write data-flow graph --- which
register does what, which cell feeds which routine --- also exact and internal, also all
64 games. T1 and T2 are everything the machine \emph{is}; they need no external label.

\emph{Tier~3 (T3)} is different in kind, and that difference is the point. T3 attaches a
\emph{game-concept} name to a cell or feature --- ``the ball's $x$ position,'' ``the
score'' --- and such names are \emph{not} computable from the substrate alone, because
they are facts about what the program \emph{means}, not about what it \emph{does}. We
import T3 from two public sources and then make them causal. AtariARI provides labels for
22 games, read from commented disassemblies and served one label per
frame~\cite{anand2019unsupervised}; its raw RAM coordinates are not aligned to the
rendered position. OCAtari covers more than 40 games and, in its RAM mode, applies the
render-time offsets that fix that misalignment~\cite{delfosse2023ocatari}. Our procedure
is then: (1) import both as \emph{candidate} labels; (2) \emph{verify each causally} by
setting the byte, re-rendering, and confirming on the bit-exact framebuffer that the
named object moves to the predicted place --- which upgrades a correlational label to a
verified-causal one and auto-corrects the offsets; and (3) \emph{discover} further labels
by RAM-to-framebuffer correlation followed by an intervention sweep. The headline
\emph{core set} is six games --- Pong, Breakout, Space Invaders, Seaquest, Ms.\ Pac-Man,
and Q*bert --- chosen because each carries \emph{both} AtariARI and OCAtari candidate
sources, all six lie in the Paper-1 bit-exact roster, and together they span paddle-and-ball,
shooter-with-shields, resource-meter, multi-agent-pursuit, and discrete-grid game logic.
Remaining games of the roster form a breadth set scored on T1/T2 only.

We are deliberate about what this buys us, and what it does not. \emph{All} T3 labels are
external and only \emph{verified}, never \emph{discovered from scratch}; a method that
matches a supplied concept has \emph{aligned} to a given meaning, not recovered it. This
is exactly why a probe can show that information is \emph{present} without showing it is
\emph{used}~\cite{hewitt2019designing}, and why our intervention test --- which asks
whether the byte \emph{causes} the named object to move --- is strictly stronger than
decoding it. Recovering the program's documentation and design from the bare machine is a
separate problem, and it is the subject of the companion paper, not this one.

\subsection{When is an explanation right? The F\,$\wedge$\,S\,$\wedge$\,M triad}\label{sec:triad}

A faithful attribution is not yet an understanding, and to make that statement a
measurement rather than an opinion we need an operational criterion for correctness. We
adopt one in the spirit of Marr's levels of analysis~\cite{marr1982vision} and of Jonas
and Kording's warning that finding structure is not the same as
explaining it~\cite{jonas2017could}. We call an explanation $\hat{E}$ of how an output
$y$ arises \emph{right} if and only if it is Faithful, Sufficient, and Minimal,
\begin{equation}
  \text{right} \;=\; \mathrm{F} \,\wedge\, \mathrm{S} \,\wedge\, \mathrm{M},
  \label{eq:triad}
\end{equation}
which reads: an explanation must name the true causes, must be able to regenerate the
behavior, and must do so at the right level with no spurious parts. The three axes are
defined against the oracle, not against any method under test, which is what keeps the
criterion non-circular.

\paragraph{Faithfulness (F).}
The claimed causes are the \emph{true} causes. We score $\hat{E}$ against the intervention
oracle of Eq.~\eqref{eq:doy} by correlation, sign and magnitude agreement, deletion and
insertion area-under-curve, and precision at $k$ against the true causal top-$k$. F is the
field's usual quality criterion, and on our machine --- uniquely --- it can actually be
checked.

\paragraph{Sufficiency (S).}
The explanation could \emph{regenerate} the behavior: it predicts $y$ under interventions
it has not seen. Crucially, we score S \emph{against the exact intervention oracle},
applying a held-out $\mathrm{do}(u)$, predicting $y$ from $\hat{E}$, and comparing to the
true $y$ on the bit-exact re-run. We deliberately do \emph{not} define S through
interchange interventions or scrubbing, which are themselves mechanistic-interpretability
methods under test in Phase~C; using them to score S would make the criterion circular.

\paragraph{Minimality (M).}
The explanation is parsimonious, has no spurious parts, and lives at the algorithmic
level --- the registers, RAM cells, opcodes, and program variables that constitute the
machine's own module decomposition. M asks whether $\hat{E}$ matches the known structure
of the computation, not merely whether it is small.

Two reporting axes carry the headline comparison: \emph{faithfulness} (F, against the
ground truth) on the horizontal, and \emph{human plausibility} on the vertical. The
plausibility axis is a documented method-tradition proxy, not a human-subjects
measurement, and we report it as such; its only job is to expose the \emph{danger zone} ---
explanations with high plausibility but low faithfulness, which look convincing and are
wrong. The separation of F (which the wiring satisfies) from S and M (which the
\emph{computation} must satisfy) is what lets us name, later, the residual that
faithfulness alone leaves behind.

\subsection{Methods under test, games, and aggregation}\label{sec:scoring}

We turn the toolkit of three traditions onto the same machine and score every method by
the triad of Eq.~\eqref{eq:triad}. The \emph{neuroscience battery} (Phase~A) runs the
classical operators --- connectomics and data-flow recovery, single-unit lesions, tuning
curves, pairwise and global correlation structure, pooled-activity spectra, Granger
causality, dimensionality reduction, and whole-state recording --- and scores each
against the known mechanism, quantifying Kording's lesson and fixing the
low-faithfulness baseline~\cite{jonas2017could}. The \emph{attribution/XAI battery}
(Phase~B) explains a chosen output from its causes with gradient saliency and its
variants, integrated gradients, occlusion, perturbation masks, randomized masking, LIME,
and Shapley-style attribution~\cite{simonyan2014deep,sundararajan2017axiomatic,zeiler2014visualizing,fong2017interpretable,petsiuk2018rise,ribeiro2016why,lundberg2017unified};
several popular methods --- Grad-CAM and its variants, attention
rollout~\cite{selvaraju2017gradcam,chattopadhyay2018gradcampp,abnar2020quantifying} ---
require neural layers or a learned policy and are recorded as \emph{not applicable} to a
non-neural machine. The \emph{mechanistic-interpretability battery} (Phase~C) runs
activation patching and causal mediation, interchange interventions and distributed
alignment search, attribution and path patching, automatic circuit discovery, sparse
autoencoders, causal scrubbing, linear probing with control tasks, and the logit/tuned
lens~\cite{vig2020investigating,meng2022locating,geiger2021causal,geiger2023finding,nanda2023attribution,wang2022interpretability,conmy2023towards,cunningham2023sparse,chan2022causal,alain2017understanding,hewitt2019designing,belrose2023eliciting}.

All faithfulness scoring runs on the six core games of Section~\ref{sec:tiers}; the
oracle's re-runs are exact on every one of them. Cell- and pixel-level scores (correlation,
deletion/insertion area-under-curve, precision at $k$) are taken against T1 and need no
T3; only object- and concept-level scores --- pointing games, object-level
counterfactuals, feature-to-variable matching --- draw on the external T3 labels, and we
flag every such score as alignment-to-given, not discovery. For the cross-tradition
comparison we aggregate the committed per-game records into one row per method and place
all methods on the shared faithfulness-versus-plausibility axes (Fig.~\ref{fig:platform}
gives the schematic; the populated leaderboard is reported in the Results). The headline
contrast is computed on the position regime, where the naive gradient is provably zero: in
that regime causal and intervention methods average a faithfulness of $0.41$ against
$0.07$ for gradient and correlational methods, a gap of $0.34$, while the per-method
extreme places an exact causal method (activation patching) at faithfulness $1.000$ and
the field's default saliency at $0.000$ on all six games --- with the popular method
nonetheless carrying the higher plausibility proxy ($0.9$ versus $0.5$). These are the
numbers against which the semantic gap is then measured.
