We ask whether explainable AI understands anything. On the fully known, bit-exact,
differentiable Atari~2600, where the true cause of any output is computable by
exact intervention, we score 31 interpretability methods against ground truth.
Causal and intervention methods are faithful, while naive-gradient saliency is
plausible but provably wrong --- zero on discrete position outputs ($0.000$
versus activation patching $1.000$). Yet faithfulness is not the dividing line.
Even at the oracle ceiling, every method delivers causal and mechanical relations,
not semantics. A graph-correct circuit need not reproduce behaviour (sufficiency
$0.44$ on Breakout); a feature that matches a named variable can be causally
inert (matched fraction $1.0$, faithfulness $0.04$ on Pong). The gap is universal:
meaning is not recoverable from mechanism at all. IEEE~610.12 defines software as
code, documentation, and data. Interpretability recovers code-level mechanism;
the documentation and data --- the semantics --- are a separate object,
unreachable from mechanism alone. The reference for meaning is behavioural and
lives in the external record, not in the wiring. Explainable AI does not
understand a thing.
