## Result (jutari, σ=8 raw-byte units, N=50, f120+30, 6 core games)
Content path (one-hot RAM read; gradient ALIVE) faithfulness vs the intervention oracle:
| game | RAM | corr | p@3 | del_auc | ins_auc | Δcorr(smooth) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| pong | 54 | 0.407 | 0.667 | 0.572 | 0.214 | 0.0 |
| breakout | 30 | 0.750 | 1.000 | 0.025 | 0.925 | 0.0 |
| space_invaders | 81 | 0.215 | 0.333 | 0.024 | 0.503 | 0.0 |
| seaquest | 118 | 0.019 | 0.667 | NaN | NaN | 0.0 |
| ms_pacman | 16 | 0.811 | 1.000 | 0.012 | 0.962 | 0.0 |
| qbert | 39 | 0.999 | 1.000 | 0.012 | 0.964 | 0.0 |

Position contrast (`ball_pixel`): SmoothGrad VANISHES on all 6 (sg_max=0, corr=0) — the
§1 "plausible ≠ faithful" failure; the intervention oracle is the sole truth there.
Harness positive control (oracle-as-method) corr=1 / precision@k=1 on every content path.
σ/N robustness sweep (`--sigma-sweep`, σ∈{0,4,8,16,32}×N∈{1,25,50,100}): content corr is
INVARIANT (max|Δcorr|=0) — smoothing denoises the visual map but does NOT change
faithfulness (soft_ram_peek's one-hot ∂y/∂u is constant in the noise). The headline finding.
