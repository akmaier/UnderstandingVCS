## Notes / handoff
experiment_design.md §6 (row "ACDC") + §7. T1/T2 scoring (no T3).

ACDC = resample-ablation edge pruning over the dense candidate-cell data-flow graph:
ablate each source (overwrite with a corrupted value — donor where it diverges, else
base+17) → 1 real-ROM re-run/source → effect(i→j)=|child_j(ablate i)−child_j(clean)|;
discovered circuit at τ = {effect > τ}; τ sweep = ROC frontier (0 extra re-runs). Ground
truth = the exhaustive exact-oracle inter-cell data-flow (A1_connectomics.true_dataflow_graph,
do-set 0/base+17/base+37, full horizon). Scrubbing = resample-ablate all non-circuit parents
of the best-F1 circuit, re-run, fraction of cell readouts preserved. Positive control =
oracle-as-ACDC (exhaustive do-set, τ=0) must score F1=1.

Per-game best-F1 (f30+30, P/R/F1 | ROC-AUC | scrub | F=F1 S=scrub M=1−overclaim):

Story (Partial→Succeed, as predicted §7): the single-value cheap resample recovers some
circuits perfectly (qbert, breakout) but suffers a recall deficit (space_invaders, seaquest)
and on pong/ms_pacman the resampled cell is re-derived/clobbered before it propagates to any
other candidate cell within the 30-frame horizon → no off-diagonal effect (disc=0, honest,
not a bug). The exhaustive oracle (occlude→0 + base+37 + full horizon) catches what the
single value misses — precision is always 1.0 (M=1.0: no spurious wiring). Goal state IS the
ground truth, so the gap is purely the cheap-ablation faithfulness deficit.
