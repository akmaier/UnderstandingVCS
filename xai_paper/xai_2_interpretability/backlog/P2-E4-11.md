## Result (measured, N=512 coalitions, target_frame=120, horizon=30, seed=0)
KernelSHAP / Shapley sampling (Lundberg & Lee 2017; Štrumbelj & Kononenko 2014):
sample coalitions from the Shapley kernel, mask the absent candidate cells to 0,
RE-RUN the real ROM for each unique coalition, solve the efficiency-constrained
weighted least squares for the SHAP values φ. Budget capped at N=512 sampled
coalitions/output (de-duplicated to the unique coalitions actually re-run + the 2
pinned endpoints f(∅)=y(all-masked), f(full)=y(intact)); ≈ 4022 real re-runs over
the 6 games. Efficiency axiom Σφ = f(full)−f(∅) holds to ≤4e-14 on every output.
Content-path corr vs oracle: pong 0.63, breakout 0.75, space_invaders 0.49,
seaquest 0.00, ms_pacman 0.81, qbert 1.00. The §7 "Partial/Fail — approximations
diverge" prediction in numbers: seaquest's content byte is driven by a SET-mode
oracle cause (base+17) the occlude→0 SHAP baseline cannot reach ⇒ honest corr=0.0
(harness oracle-as-method corr=1.0, so it is a true measurement of a genuine
baseline-dependence failure, not a harness artefact). §7 contrast vs IG/saliency:
KernelSHAP still produces a real attribution on the POSITION output (pong 0.67,
space_invaders 0.47, qbert 0.53) where the gradient methods vanish.
