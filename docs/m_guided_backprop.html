Reading this example's causal region

This example explains the content of RAM $36 (byte 54) — the most causally-active concept byte at this state. Its strongest true-causes are: RAM $31 (17% of its footprint sits in the score band, the rest in the play area); RAM $36 (the play area — ball / paddles). The score digits appear in the region because RAM $31 reaches them: perturbing it over the 30-frame NOOP window changes the game outcome, and hence the score — a downstream effect, not direct rendering.

What it does

Backpropagation that suppresses negative signals, sharpening the saliency map. Includes the Adebayo et al. 2018 sanity check; scored against the oracle.

How it's scored

Pearson correlation with the oracle's causal map. It partly tracks the oracle on content (colour) outputs — the example shown — but on discrete sprite-position outputs the naive gradient vanishes to exactly 0, and it fails the Adebayo et al. 2018 sanity check, so its cross-game audit faithfulness stays low.

The score is measured against the §1 intervention oracle — never against another interpretability method. F (faithful) is always vs the oracle; see the execution stack.
