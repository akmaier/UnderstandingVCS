### air_raid  (24px, frame 1, row 221) — player/color_only [high]

# Render-Divergence Synthesis: jutari vs xitari (20 games)

## Executive Summary

Of the 20 framebuffer divergences, **16 collapse into just three TIA mechanisms** — and they are not independent. Twelve of them are variants of a single underlying defect: **jutari renders each scanline once from end-of-line latched register state, instead of compositing per-color-clock as the beam passes.** This "scanline-snapshot vs racing-the-beam" gap manifests as GRP/VDELP shadow-latch errors, mid-scanline RESP/HMOVE reposition loss, sub-copy GRP-latch granularity, leading-edge off-by-one, and CTRLPF/RESMP gates applied to the whole row. The single highest-leverage move is **not** to chase the exotic groups but to make the player/object compositor honor per-color-clock register state — however, that is a deep, high-regression-risk rewrite. The pragmatically correct **first fix** is the narrower, high-confidence, low-blast-radius **mid-scanline position-strobe group** (berzerk, robotank, up_n_down, carnival, and likely defender), because RESP0/RESP1 strobes are currently applied in-place to the entire line in one localized code path (`tia_poke!` W_RESP0/W_RESP1, TIA.jl:724–732) and can be deferred/segmented without touching the GRP serializer that every pixel-exact game depends on. Four findings are flagged as **probe-vs-render disagreements** and should be triaged for a possible probe bug before being trusted as emulation defects.

## Group 1 — GRP / VDELP graphics shadow-latch (the "delayed player graphic" family)

**Games:** amidar, atlantis, defender, demon_attack, name_this_game, solaris — plus centipede and jamesbond as sub-copy/edge variants, and pooyan as the pure leading-edge case. Core VDELP members: **amidar, atlantis, defender, demon_attack, name_this_game, solaris** (6).

**Common mechanism.** All sit on a scanline where VDELPx is set or transitioning 1→0, with NUSIZ=0x03 (three-copies-close) and a mid-line GRP write. xitari serializes each player copy from the graphics byte that was *effective at that copy's start*, selecting the VDELP shadow (delayed) buffer when VDELPx=1. jutari instead composites the **freshly-written live GRP** for the whole scanline (or selects the wrong buffer at the VDELP transition), so it lights edge bits / extra copies / wrong copies that xitari leaves dark. Every member reports **GRP write-activation cc matching xitari cc-for-cc** — the defect is uniformly *which buffer* is clocked, never *when* the write lands.

**Would one fix close all of them?** Plausibly the VDELP core (6 games) yes — a correct shadow-register model (latch new GRPx into a shadow on write; serialize the previous shadow under VDELPx; clock the shadow swap on the partner-GRP write) is exactly xitari's `grp/grp_shadow` semantics and would address amidar, atlantis, defender, demon_attack, name_this_game, solaris together. centipede/jamesbond/pooyan are a *different sub-issue* (per-copy latch granularity and leading-edge boundary, see Group 4) and would likely NOT be fixed by the shadow-buffer change alone.

**Risk: HIGH.** This rewrites the central player serializer (`_object_pixel_sets` / `_overlay_player!`, TIA.jl ~894–905, ~1500). That path renders *every* player in *every* pixel-exact game. Any error in shadow-swap edge timing risks regressing the 44/64 screen-exact games and is the most likely group to trip a revert. High value (6–9 games) but high effort and high blast radius.

## Group 2 — Mid-scanline object-position strobes not rendered per-pixel (RESP/HMOVE-in-place)

**Games:** berzerk, robotank, up_n_down, carnival — and defender's *positional* component overlaps here. (4–5)

**Common mechanism.** The ROM strobes RESP0/RESP1 (and cycles COLUPx) multiple times per scanline to paint repositioned/recolored copies ("rainbow road", multiplexed sprites). jutari's `tia_poke!` handles W_RESP0/W_RESP1 (**TIA.jl:724–732**) by *immediately overwriting* `tia.p0_x/p1_x` via `_resp_player_position(beam_cc)` — these are **not** deferred into `pending_writes` like PF/GRP/COLU/NUSIZ. The whole line is then rendered once from the *final* position, so only the last strobe survives: berzerk shows a ~+45px shift, robotank loses 2 of 3 copies (18px missing), up_n_down drops the leading COLUP0=0x14@cc9 and all four RESP strobes, carnival emits a same-line RESP phantom with stale color.

**Would one fix close all of them?** Yes, with good odds. Deferring/segmenting RESPx (and the in-place RESMx/RESBL/HMOVE position mutations at TIA.jl:733–771) so position changes take effect only for beam pixels right of the strobe's color-clock — mirroring xitari's `updateFrame(clock+11)` before `myPOSP0 = newx` — addresses berzerk, robotank, carnival, up_n_down and defender's shift in one mechanism. carnival additionally needs the RESP-to-first-pixel start delay (suppress the drawing copy on the strobe line), a small adjunct in the same path.

**Risk: MEDIUM, but LOWER blast radius than Group 1.** The change is localized to the RESP/RESM/RESBL/HMOVE position-write handlers and a per-segment position application in the render loop — it does **not** touch the GRP serializer's graphics-byte selection that the pixel-exact games depend on. Games that strobe RESP only once per line are unaffected because "segment right of the single strobe" == "whole line." That containment is what makes this the safest multi-game win.

## Group 3 — Per-color-clock register gate applied scanline-globally (CTRLPF reflect, RESMP suppress, PF2 boundary)

**Games:** wizard_of_wor (CTRLPF reflect), ice_hockey (RESMP0 suppress), tutankham (PF2 scanline-boundary straddle). Asterix's RESMP/ENAM candidate is adjacent. (3–4)

**Common mechanism.** A control bit is read *live / end-of-scanline* and applied to the entire row, but its write activates *mid-line*: wizard_of_wor mirrors the whole right half because CTRLPF.D0 reflect is read globally though it only activates at cc=213 (cols 145–147 should still be non-reflected); ice_hockey suppresses a missile for the whole row though RESMP0=0x02 only activates at cc=210, after the beam painted the missile at cc~140; tutankham defers a PF2=0x00 write to the wrong scanline because activation_clock = beam_cc+delay straddles the 136→137 boundary and is never wrapped into the next line.

**Would one fix close all of them?** No — these share a *philosophy* (thread the control bit through per-color-clock activation like PF0/PF1/PF2 already are) but each is a distinct register path: CTRLPF reflect-select in `_object_pixel_sets` (TIA.jl:1500–1502) + `render_pixel` (TIA.jl:1456); RESMP0 D1 gate in `_overlay_missile!` (TIA.jl:1312) / `_missile_set` (TIA.jl:1563); and the scanline-boundary wrap in `tia_poke!` defer math (TIA.jl:648). Three separate small fixes, each individually high-confidence.

**Risk: LOW–MEDIUM per fix, and they are independent.** Each is narrow and self-contained — good "singles" to bank after the lead fix. tutankham's boundary-wrap is the most general (it fixes a class of straddling writes) but also the subtlest to get right.

## Group 4 — Sub-copy GRP latch granularity / leading-edge off-by-one

**Games:** centipede, jamesbond, pooyan. (3)

**Common mechanism.** A mid-line GRP write activates *inside* a NUSIZ multi-copy span, and jutari's render loop recomputes the **entire multi-copy mask from the live register forward** at the activation clock (TIA.jl ~894–905), lighting pixels in a copy whose serial shifter was loaded with the *old* GRP at copy-start. pooyan is the pure case: an inclusive `activation_clock <= c` boundary lights the rising-edge pixel one color-clock early vs xitari's exclusive `updateFrame` upper bound. centipede/jamesbond are the same boundary expressed inside a copy.

**Would one fix close all of them?** Possibly — latch GRPx per-copy at the copy's leftmost beam position (xitari `computePlayerMaskTable` semantics) and/or make the deferred-write apply boundary strictly-before (`<` not `<=`) for the live-GRP path. This is closely entangled with Group 1's serializer and would best be tackled *with* it, not separately.

**Risk: HIGH (shared serializer) for the per-copy latch; LOW for the pooyan-only `<`/`<=` boundary tweak** — but the boundary tweak risks shifting every deferred GRP edge by one clock across all games, so it must be gated on the full screen sweep.

## Group 5 — Missile/ball HMOVE per-scanline motion accumulation

**Games:** journey_escape (missile-0 stuck; 319px), elevator_action (RESMP0 release-reposition off by +40). (2)

**Common mechanism.** Object-X is updated only at explicit HMOVE strobes rather than free-running per scanline. journey_escape holds HMM0=0x60 with HMOVE strobed ~5×/frame; xitari's missile counter drifts every line (diagonal sweep) while jutari pins it (vertical stripes). elevator_action's RESMP0-release snaps the missile to the HMOVE-shifted `p0_x` instead of xitari's player reset-base `myPOSP0`.

**Would one fix close both?** No — distinct (continuous HMOVE accumulation vs reset-base tracking). Both medium-confidence, both isolated, low cross-game value. Defer. (journey_escape also exposes a **tool bug**: render_diff.py hardcodes 210 rows but the game is 230 tall — fix the harness height regardless.)

## Group 6 — Static analog-pin default (singleton)

**Game:** air_raid (24px/frame, pure bit-7 color). (1)

**Mechanism.** jutari's INPT0 paddle-pot default returns D7=1; xitari's joystick drives it low (D7=0). air_raid reads INPT0 via `LDA $58` and routes D7 into COLUP0/P1. **High confidence, trivial, fully isolated, RAM-safe** ($58/$ba are transient TIA-pin reads, re-zeroed before the RAM compare). Fix: default INPT0..INPT3 to D7=0 for joystick controllers (TIA.jl ~line405 inpt init + tia_peek; mirror in jaxtari system.py). Excellent cheap single — bank it alongside the lead fix.

## Recommended FIRST fix (detailed) — Group 2: defer/segment mid-scanline RESP (and RESM/RESBL/HMOVE) position strobes

**Why this one.** Four-to-five games (berzerk, robotank, up_n_down, carnival, defender-shift), three of them **high** confidence (berzerk, robotank, up_n_down, carnival). The root cause is a single, well-localized behavior: position strobes mutate object-X *in place* and bypass the deferred-activation pipeline that PF/GRP/COLU/NUSIZ already use. It does **not** touch the GRP graphics-byte serializer that the pixel-exact corpus depends on, so the regression surface is small and self-limiting.

**Current defective behavior.** In `tia_poke!`, the W_RESP0/W_RESP1 handlers (**TIA.jl:724–732**) immediately overwrite `tia.p0_x` / `tia.p1_x` via `_resp_player_position(beam_cc)`; W_RESM0/W_RESM1/W_RESBL and the immediate HMOVE motion handlers (**TIA.jl:733–771**) likewise mutate obj-X in place. The whole scanline is then composited once in `tia_advance!` (**TIA.jl:884–926**) from a single `cached_sets` built off the *final* obj-X. xitari instead calls `updateFrame(clock+11)` (e.g. case 0x10/0x11, TIA.cxx:2250–2289) to paint the framebuffer up to the current beam **before** assigning the new position, so each strobe's copies persist.

**Concrete fix to try.**
1. Convert the position strobes from in-place mutation into **per-color-clock activations**: when RESPx/RESMx/RESBL (and the immediate-path HMOVE motion) fire at `beam_cc`, push a position-change activation at `activation_clock = beam_cc + <reset delay>` into the same `pending_writes` queue the deferred registers already use, rather than writing `tia.p0_x` directly.
2. In the render loop (`tia_advance!`, TIA.jl ~884–905), apply each position activation when `activation_clock <= c`, recomputing the object's copy positions for **pixels from that color-clock forward only**. Pixels left of the strobe retain the prior position — exactly the per-segment behavior xitari gets from `updateFrame` before the store. This is the same per-color-clock comb the HMOVE path already uses for motion; extend it to RESP/RESM/RESBL.
3. **carnival adjunct (same path):** honor xitari's RESP-to-first-pixel start delay — suppress the drawing copy on the scanline where RESPx is strobed so the player counter begins the copy on the following beam pass, eliminating the same-line phantom (and its tell-tale stale COLUP color).

**Why it won't regress single-strobe games.** When a line has exactly one RESPx strobe, "render the segment right of the strobe at the new position" is identical to "render the whole line at the new position" — the current behavior. The deferred-activation insertion reuses an existing, sweep-validated queue, so its boundary semantics are already exercised by the 44/64 screen-exact games. The change is invisible to any game that does not multiplex positions mid-line.

**Gate.** Full 64-ROM RAM[64/64] + screen sweep, run **alone** (per the xdist-hang note — heavy gates must not run concurrently with the sweep/workflow). Any RAM regression or any currently-pixel-exact screen game dropping = revert. After it lands, bank G6 (air_raid) and the three independent G3 fixes (wizard_of_wor reflect, ice_hockey RESMP, tutankham PF2-boundary) as separate low-risk commits before attempting the high-risk G1 shadow-buffer rework.

## Probe-vs-render disagreements (possible PROBE bugs, triage before trusting as emulation defects)

These five report jutari's own object-priority/pixel-set probe disagreeing with its framebuffer — the render path draws an object the probe says isn't there. That pattern can equally indicate a **probe bug** (probe samples end-of-line state while render committed mid-line) as a render bug; verify the probe before spending fix effort:

- **amidar** — ju-set probe says **bg** at cols 83–85, framebuffer draws player0. (G1)
- **asterix** — probe says **bg** at col 92, framebuffer draws missile-0; *and* the missile-enable feeding the compositor disagrees with the state feeding priority. (G3/missile)
- **centipede** — probe says **bg** at cols 119/120 (later GRP0=0x00@cc205 cleared the set) yet pixels were committed at cc187. (G4) — this one is explained by the render committing earlier than the probe samples, i.e. likely a *real* mid-line render/probe-sampling skew, not a pure probe bug.
- **pooyan** — probe says **bg** at col 83, framebuffer draws p1. (G4) — consistent with the inclusive-boundary off-by-one being a *real* render bug the probe correctly disputes.
- **solaris** — end-of-line p1 pixel-set is empty (GRP1=0/VDELP1=0) yet framebuffer shows color 30; the render latched 0x1e mid-line, the probe did not. (G1)

Triage rule: where the disagreement is explained by *render committing a mid-line value that the end-of-line probe no longer reflects* (centipede, pooyan, solaris, amidar), the **render** is the suspect and the probe is merely sampling late — these are real defects. asterix is the one where the compositor and priority logic disagree on a *carried-over enable state* on the same line, which most warrants a dedicated probe-vs-compositor consistency check before committing a fix.
